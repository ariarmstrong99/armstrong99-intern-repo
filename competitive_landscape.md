
What makes Focus Bear different from these apps? 

In contrast to freedom, cold turkey, tiimo, and routinery, focus bear is attempting to address the behavioural loop that causes neurodivergent people to have their productivity disrupted at specific points in time during the day. 
A lot the competitors such as freedom and cold turkey tend to operate in order to stop distractions from occurring. 
However, this relies on the user to actually decide what they ought to be doing and this ultimately comes down to how much clarity a user has in terms of what they need to be doing in the present moment. 
Whereas tiimo and routinery tend to focus on helping users become more organised and following routines. 
Focus bear on the other hand tries to consolidate the gap between that exists between a persons intent and their ability to successfully execute by assisting neurodivergent people to decide what they  are going to do, how they are going to do it and how the are going to continue to do it. 
This is particularly important for neurodivergent users, more specifically for users with ADHD that struggle with executive dysfunction. 
These individuals do not struggle with being unable to plan or a lack of willpower or being able to use blockers in isolation. 
The fundamental issue is that People with adhd struggle with knowing what should be done and being able to actually do it properly. 
Focus bear combines, focus sessions, distraction blockers, routines, provides mechanisms for accountability, and behavioural prompts that are designed to help individuals around that transition point. 
Another key difference is that focus bear actually treats productivity not as some abstract concept, but  as a system rooted in behaviour rather than features. 
A lot of apps allow users to create routines, block distracting apps and websites, or track their habits in isolation. 
Instead, focus bear attempts to connect all these actions together so that all the routines lead the user into focus sessions, the focus sessions in turn trigger distraction controls and the users behaviour is then reinforced through consistently executing actions daily in their life. 
Focus bear cannot be conceptualised as a blocking tool, planner or habit tracking or productivity app. Focus bear is more of an operating system for neurodivergent Individuals, particularly those with adhd, to help them move from intention to action on a repeated basis. 




If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors? 

As a user, I would choose focus bear over the other competitors since it aims to reduce the number of individual tools I have to use in order to be more productive daily. 
Freedom and cold turkey can help block distractions, but there is still another system that is needed in order to help me organise use and build my routines. 
With tiimo or routinery, I am able to structure my day successfully, but I still need another tool to help me resist the distractions once work commences. 
Focus bear successfully combines these capabilities into a single workflow, and allows me to plan the day, build routines, do focus sessions, and also successfully block all distractions without constantly being forced to switch between various applications and tools. 
I would want to choose focus bear because executive functioning due to adhd impacts task initiation quite badly. 
A lot of productivity tools tend to asssume that users know exactly what they need to be doing and they will then naturally start their work and be able to stay focused while they work. 
In contrast, focus bear understands that executive functioning impairs the neurodivergent users ability to get started with their work, maintain their focus, and then transition between activities that can be challenging. 
By successfully combining routines, prompts, focus sessions, and distraction blocking, it provides support to users throughout the day instead of addressing one part of the overall productivity process. 


What’s one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn’t? 

One feature that comes to mind immediately is tiimo’s visual timeline view. The app allows users to see their day as a schedule that can be structured visually. 
This makes it easier to understand what a user should be doing, what exactly is coming next, and how long an activity is expected to actually  take. 
This can be quite useful for adhd users that have time blindness and can benefit from being able to visually see how their tasks are progressing with time. 
Focus bear provides routines and focus sessions instead, but the planning capabilities are quite lacking in visual terms. 
Being able to utilise a more visually friendly and visually appealing interface that can help users gain a better understanding of their schedule at a glance and then strengthen their link between the routines, focus sessions and daily goals


Based on your research, what’s one improvement you think Focus Bear could make? 

I feel that focus bear could benefit greatly from reducing the learning curve new users need to use the app via a more guided, intuitive and progressive onboarding experience. 
In contrast to competitors like tiimo, routinery, freedom and cold turkey, focus bear tends to offer a much broader range of functionality. 
It successfully combines routines, focus sessions, distraction blocking, formation and also accountability features all packaged into one app. 
Packing these many features into an app can sometimes make a product feel a lot more complex for a first time user in particular. 
A lot of the competitor apps, such as freedom and tiimo are much more intuitive and easier for a first time user to understand. 
This is because the focus is more on one singular issue. For example, freedom tends to focus on blocking distractions, while tiimo tends to focus on planning and time management. 
Whereas routinery tends to focus on routines. Due to this, users can experience the value of the product much more faster and in a more pronounced manner, without being forced into learning multiple systems. 
Focus bear can improve drastically by ensuring that users are gradually introduced to its core features. 
Insteadd of overwhelming users with setting up routines, and block settings without Proper guidance early on in their experience, focus bear needs to first guide users properly towards understand how things work. 
For example, “what is the difference between habit, task and routine”‘and then properly guiding users to build routines after they understand how the product works instead of taking them through an onboarding that is unintuitive, frustrating, too long, and fails to make the value proposition clear to users and once this is complete, just leave the user on the homepage to figure things out themselves instead of taking a more hands on guided and intuitive approach. 
Taking this approach would reduce cognitive overwhelm for users, help them understand how the app works and what value it can provide, without frustrating them mentally and emotionally.
