What are common cyber security threats in a remote work environment?

Cyber security threats have become more prevalent and pervasive over the past 10 years. As remote work increases globally, this poses serious cyber security risks due to the ongoing threat of email phishing attacks, malware, issues caused due to wifi networks that are unsecured, data breaches and weak passwords leading to scams. 
Human error is a major factor and attackers will seek to exploit human error to their advantage. For example, a team member May install a productivity tool that looks promising third party tool on the Same device containing company data. However, such a tool could be malware in disguise and lead to secure data being leaked. 
When workers are involved in remote work, they often access systems from different parts of the world. It becomes even more important to set up strong two factor authentication systems and strong passwords to properly handle sensitive information.

What makes a strong password, and why should you use a password manager?

A strong password is a necessity with the number of security breaches that are occurring in our contemporary society. Using a strong password that is long, hard to guess and one that contains 10-18 characters is ideal. 
It is also recommended to not reuse the same password for multiple accounts. Using the same password for multiple accounts could allow attackers to breach mulltiple accounts with one attack. 
This is why using a password manager it’s important because it is not possible to remember highly complex passwords that are 10-18 characters long. 
A password manager stores passwords for different accounts and also generates strong passwords to keep your accounts secure. 

What is a two factor authentication important, and when should you enable it?

A two factor authentication gives people an added layer of security compared to traditional passwords. A two factor authentication requires a second verification after your password has been entered, such as by using an authentication app or following a login prompt on one of your other devices or email  accounts. 
This prevents people from scedsing your accounts even if they manage to somehow access your password for a particular account. Without the second factor authentication, they fail to get access to your account. A password is no longer adequate as a way to log in. 
Two factor authentication should be used on all important accounts. It is a highly effective way to prevent cybersecurity breaches.
