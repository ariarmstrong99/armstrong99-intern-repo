How can you give and receive feedback professionally? (e.g., focus on actions, not people; assume good intent; ask for clarification instead of reacting defensively) 

Receiving and giving feedback in professional setting means being able to provide constructive feedback that is going to the person improve and not focused on ad hominem attacks. For example, if a team member is struggling with a particular issue, it more conducive to help them get to the root cause of the issue and help them by giving them constructive feedback instead of labelling them “lazy”. 
Another example could be giving feedback on another team members design decisions by ensuring that is is constructive and based on evidenced based UX practices. It is not enough to simply say “the design is really bad”. 
It is Important to properly articulate with proper evidence as to why the design is “really bad”. For example, we can say “based on the design at present, it fails to adhere to these specific user experience principles and it’s failing to address the issues from the previous usability tests, maybe we can perhaps look to brainstorm again to ensure we focus on addressing the issues from the user testing results”. 
It is Important to provide steps and reasons behind how a design can be improved. 
When giving any type of feedback, it is quite important to ensure that the person is given clear steps and instructions to actually improve. 
When receiving feedback, it is Important to ask questions and get clarity on how things can be improved. 
It is unproductive to respond defensively or take constructive criticism negatively. 
However, if the feedback becomes too personal, then it is important to politely remind the person giving the feedback that it is unhelpful and inappropriate. 
For example, being told “your lazy, your not getting enough done and you can easily be replaced. You just don’t want to work” is a personal attack and entirely unhelpful to the receiving the feedback. 
Receiving feedback also means being able to discuss this feedback without judgement in an open, honest and supportive manner so that improvements and growth can occur at the fastest possible rate.
