What is a user manual for me and why do teams use them?

A “user manual for me” is essentially a document that is able to explain to team members how a person is able to work at their full potential and deliver consistent high quality work related outputs. 
It will Include information details about a persons communication style, their specific strengths, the types of supports they need and what they need in order to perform at their best level.
A lot of teams use these manuals in order to reduce the chances of misunderstandings or miscommunications from occurring. 
When such misunderstandings occur, it can take up the mental bandwidth of team members over something that may be a trivial issue that was miscommunicated. 
With a user manual approach for team members, it removes uncertainty about how each person from the team likes to communicate and how the team can maximise the productivity of each person by working with their preferred approach when it comes to key issues such as communication and working style. 


What categories are helpful to include?

Key categories that should be included in a “user manual for me” include:

My role: this will include a brief description of my current role at focus bear
Working hours and availability: this will help team members identify when I am available and when I’m have other commitments
Communication style: this will capture information how I as a person like to communicate and what would the best type of approach people could take to communicate with me. For example, Whether I prefer in person communication vs short and precise emails to discuss important issues is a detail that would go under this category.
Work habits: this will capture information about what conditions are needed that help you produce your best work. For example, do you need tasks to be defined in a certain way, do you work best with deep work focus hours or do you prefer longer days. This is the type of information under this category. 
Collaboration preferences: this will capture information about how you best collaborate with your other team members. For example, what your pet peeves are in a teamwork dynamic
 Feedback preferences:  this will contain information about how you as a team member like to receive feedback and how the feedback should be delivered to you 
Things that help me stay productive: this will have information on what specific practices, approaches, tools and supports will help you stay productive as a team member. For example, it will have information on what things distract me and how I can minimise the impact of these disruptions.
Common challenges or  frustrations:  this will have Information about things that can slow you down. 
Strengths: this will have information about the skills or qualities about you possess and that will help you provide value to the team.
Personal notes or fun facts: this contains light hearted information about things like hobbies, interests and anything else you might want to add in order for team members to better connect with you as a focus bear team member. 


What do you need from team mates do your best work?

I prefer my team mates to communicate directly, transparently and openly with myself. I do not work well when I attend long meetings as this can exhaust me mentally if the meetings are not productive and lead to drift after the meetings. 
I also work best when I have tasks that have been well defined and are clear in their instructions. Such as what deliverables are to be producted, what method/ approach should be taken for the specific task. 
The more detailed the instructions, the better. I also prefer to work long blocks of time that are uninterrupted with minimal breaks as this removes my momentum and depletes my energy with context switching. 
I am most productive when i have long blocks of time where I can get into my work deeply. I struggle with short blocks of time that require me to constantly switch context. I need team members to be patient, understanding, direct and honest.

How do you prefer to communicate?

I mainly prefer to communicate in person, in a 1 to 1 meeting or on phone. I do not prefer text or email communications as it can  lead to misunderstands for important and serious matters. However, for routine tasks, it is best to communicate via email. 
Quick checks in’s would be my most preferred method (‘via zoom or phone call)

What helps you stay focused and productive?

I can stay focused when I have minimal background noise and a quiet workspace. I struggle to work in a workspace where multiple people are talking loudly, with music playing in the background and with constant noise. 
I also prefer my workspace to by tidy, minimalistic with fewer distractions. I tend to prefer a plain, and drab room that almost seems spartan in many ways to minimise distractions. 
I also journal frequently before commencing work about my goals for the day, and how I am going to approach the work for the day. It allows me to clear my mind and focus fully on the task ahead. 
I also prefer to work long uninterrupted blocks of time, instead of one or two hours thrown in between meetings, errands and other commitments. I struggle with context switching and it can impair my ability to do deep work and focus when this occurs. 
I like to keep things simple. An almost clear table, no music, minimal external noise and nothing to distract me with.

How do you like to give and receive feedback?

I usually like to provide feedback in a direct, open, honest, compassionate and precise manner. I often emphasise constructive criticism to help others improve by developing more insights into their own behaviours and how they can improve further by auctioning certain steps. 
I am a huge proponent of positive reinforcement. I also providing feedback verbally over written format so that it feels more personable. 
It also allows me to connect with the person properly instead of doing this via email or text. In terms of receiving feedback, I prefer the feedback to be in person as well, be direct, precise and constructive. 
I am very committed to constant improvement and take feedback very seriously. For this reason, I find if any feedback were to be given it should be detailed on how I can improve and what steps would help me improve.

What are some things to know about your work style?

I often need a bit of time to process new ideas and new information. I also tend to be a deep thinker and need to take notes when new information is discussed so that I am able to process information in a better manner. 
I find journaling things to be helpful so that I can better retain the information. For example, if wireframes are being discussed on a new feature for the app, I often take notes of what team members say and I note down what I can do to make improvements to the wireframes. 
This helps me process things significantly better and helps me gets into a deeper state of focus as well when I work.  I like to brainstorm things in workshops with other team members and hear their input as well. 
If instructions are quite detailed and complex, I prefer them to be in text format, I forget things quite often and am constantly making notes.
