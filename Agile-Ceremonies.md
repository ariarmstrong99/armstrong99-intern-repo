What are agile ceremonies and what is their purpose?

 agile ceremonies are meetings that take place on a recurring basis that allow the team to plan their work, identify issues that slow down the workflow and better coordinate their progress as a team. 
 Agile ceremonies provide the team with a structured form of communication that essentially facilitates and enhances coordination. 


Agile ceremonies can consist of:

Daily standups: here the team convenes on a daily basis during business hours in order to share their progress, plans or any issues that may be hindering their progress on work related tasks.
Sprint planning scrum: these meetings occur in a team that uses a scrum framework prior to the commencement of a sprint. The product manager will discuss the tasks with the highest levels priority that need to be completed from the list of “backlog” tasks. 
The team will then discuss what tasks from the “backlog” list should be done within the upcoming sprint realistically. Once the sprint begins, the team can focus on those tasks.
Retrospective: these are meetings whereby the team is able to reflect on what has worked well, what did not as well as anticipated, and what particular changes could help improve the overall process. The overall purpose is continuous improvement.
Backlog refinement: these sessions help the tesm focus on clarifying specific requirements, the team discusses how large tasks can be broken down into multiple tasks to streamline their execution and ensure that all work is well defined with clarity before the work commenced.
Continuous prioritisation (‘kanban): under a kanban framework, sprint planning sessions do not exist. Instead team members review the backlog on a regular basis and rearrange tasks so that the important tasks stay at the very top.


How do agile teams collaborate asynchronously and across time zones?

Agile teams are able to collaborate asynchronously over multiple time zones because they often have shared documentation clarifying requirements. 
Everyone does not have to be online simultaneously and tasks are often tracked in platforms like GitHub. 
All design files are shared between members as well and any discussions that take place are often documented so that other team members not involved during the discussions can review the notes when possible.
For example, as a product design/UX intern I can share a figma file with a prototype and leave detailed notes about how the interactions should behave. 
This allows the developers to review the file at their convenience and leave comments about anything they need clarification on. 
Maintaining clear documentation of design decisions and business processes helps team members that are not based in the same location to work without being in the same time zone, during business hours in their respective nation. 
By having a clear list of tasks to focus on under a kanban system, this makes it even more streamlined.


How do agile ceremonies help with communication and alignment?

Agile ceremonies help team members with communication and helping them with alignment by creating a space for discussing issues, progress and priorities on a regular basis. 
It prevents the need to rely on communication is ad hoc and haphazard in nature, which can ultimately be far more exhausting. 
Daily standup meetings allow team members to openly discuss any issues they have encountered in order to complete the tasks that were allocated to them earlier in the week. 
It gives them an opportunity to share their progress, get feedback from other team members and refocus their attention. In contrast, a retrospective session is where team members can reflect on what went well and what needs improvement in a manner that is quite structured in nature. 
Agile ceremonies can be useful because they enhance the overall level of communication, prevent miscommunication from taking place and make sure that the designers and developers are all making progress.
